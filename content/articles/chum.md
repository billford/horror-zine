The plot revolves around newlyweds Tina (Alice Eve) and Tom (Eric Michael Cole), who join friends Rachinda (Sarah Siadat), Sadie (Elle Haymond), Roy (Jim Klock), Rick (Johnny Gaffney), and Britney (Lisa Yaro) on a luxurious Mediterranean yacht. What should be an idyllic vacation takes a grim turn when the group finds themselves besieged by both a predatory shark and a psychopathic killer in their midst.

The tagline, "There's no hiding in the sea," succinctly encapsulates the predicament of the characters. In this claustrophobic setting, every movement, every shadow, becomes a potential threat—be it from beneath the waves or from one of the passengers themselves.

Director Zuck’s vision is clear: he wants to evoke both the visceral fear of shark attacks and the psychological horror of being stalked by an unseen killer. The script, co-written by Dick Grunert, James Kondelik, Ryan R. Johnson, and Joe Leone, attempts to blend these elements seamlessly, but ultimately finds itself stretched thin.

One cannot help but notice that "Chum" struggles with pacing and character development. This often results in characters feeling like placeholders rather than fully realized individuals. Tina and Tom’s newlywed relationship is hastily sketched, leaving viewers with little emotional investment in their survival.
